Summary

Donald Norman's critique: designers have become 'applied behavioral scientists' but are 'woefully undereducated.' Design schools don't train in behavioral sciences, scientific method, or interlocking complexities. The series aims to ground design in empirical research and generative concepts.

This section establishes the intellectual foundation, situating design as both an ancient human practice and a modern discipline requiring theoretical depth alongside practical skill.

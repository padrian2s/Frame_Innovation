Commentary

Summary

Frame creation challenges conventional rationality. Lakoff's five aspects: rational thought is (1) conscious, (2) universal, (3) literal, (4) disembodied, (5) dispassionate. Frame creation involves intuitive, situated, metaphorical, embodied, engaged thinking.

Key Patterns

Seeing, thinking, and doing differently are interrelated. All three must be addressed. The challenges reflect deep assumptions about rationality, identity, and practice.

Practical Applications

Address three challenges simultaneously: create opportunities for seeing through others' eyes, introduce broader notions of valid reasoning, and protect experimental spaces for new approaches.

Real-World Example

Standard business planning follows conventional rationality: conscious (no intuition), universal (one-size-fits-all), literal (metrics only), disembodied (spreadsheets), dispassionate (data-driven). Frame creation challenges every aspect.

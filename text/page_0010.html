Summary

Ten challenges unite design professions: three performance (acting on the physical world, addressing human needs, generating the built environment), four substantive (ambiguous boundaries, large-scale frames, complex environments, information exceeding physical value), and three contextual (cross-organizational, multi-stakeholder, multi-level demands).

Key Patterns

This section establishes the intellectual foundation, situating design as both an ancient human practice and a modern discipline requiring theoretical depth alongside practical skill.

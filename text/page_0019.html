Design Thinking and Framing

Commentary

Summary

Organizations are turning to 'design thinking' for help, but most focus on solution generation rather than the key ability: framing. The creation of new 'frames' is the special element of designers' problem-solving. Expert practitioners have developed sophisticated multilayered practices for creating new frames.

Key Patterns

Conventional problem-solving fails not because practitioners are incompetent, but because problems have fundamentally changed. The five syndromes are deeply human patterns that become traps in complex, networked environments.

Practical Applications

Check for the five syndromes in your organization: Lone Warrior? Freeze the World? Self-Made Box? Rational High Ground? Identity around practices? Recognizing these patterns is the first step.

Series Foreword — Design as Practice

Commentary

Summary

The series foreword traces design's origins from Homo habilis 2.5 million years ago to modern practice. Design is fundamentally about service — conceiving, planning, and creating things that meet human needs. The word 'design' entered English as a verb in 1548, emphasizing its nature as deliberate planning toward desired outcomes.

Key Patterns

This section establishes the intellectual foundation, situating design as both an ancient human practice and a modern discipline requiring theoretical depth alongside practical skill.

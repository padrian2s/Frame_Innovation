Series Foreword — Design as Process and Noun

Commentary

Summary

Herbert Simon's definition: to design is to 'devise courses of action aimed at changing existing situations into preferred ones.' The foreword catalogs design disciplines — from industrial and graphic to systems and urban design — noting that common concerns at disciplinary boundaries create bridges between them.

Key Patterns

This section establishes the intellectual foundation, situating design as both an ancient human practice and a modern discipline requiring theoretical depth alongside practical skill.

Chapter 6: The Open, Complex Organization — Routine Reaction

Commentary

Summary

The 'routine reaction': organizations apply conventional methods to new problems. Not irrational — past practices were refined through experience. But they become traps when problems change character.

Routine Reaction: An organization's default of applying conventional methods to new problems, regardless of appropriateness.

Key Patterns

Organizational adoption requires structural, cultural, and identity-level changes. Surface adoption without structural change produces no lasting results.

Practical Applications

Identify one persistent problem conventional approaches have failed to solve. Use it as a pilot, with a diverse team and adequate time. Build the case for frame creation from evidence.

Real-World Example

Retailer facing declining traffic: the routine reaction cuts prices or redesigns layout. Frame innovation questions whether 'retail' is still the right frame for the space. What if it's a community hub that happens to sell products?

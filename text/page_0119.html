Principle 1: Keep It Open

Commentary

Summary

Principle 1: Keep it open. Resist premature closure. The natural tendency is to converge quickly. Extended openness — uncomfortable as it is — enables genuinely new frames to emerge.

Premature Closure: Converging on a solution before fully exploring the problem situation. Frame creation requires tolerating ambiguity longer than comfortable.

Key Patterns

Principles emphasize openness, depth, collaboration, and attraction. Quality depends on investigation and engagement more than creative brilliance. Thoroughness enables creativity.

Practical Applications

Practice the attract principle: design solutions people want to participate in. Ask: would stakeholders choose this voluntarily? If not, reconsider the frame.

Real-World Example

In product development, premature closure = committing to features before understanding needs. Teams feel pressure to 'ship.' Staying open longer feels inefficient but consistently produces better outcomes.

The Five Syndromes: Lone Warrior

Commentary

Summary

Five syndromes of conventional organizations: (1) 'Lone Warrior' — one party 'owns' the problem and drives it alone. (2) 'Freeze the World' — need to stop the world and isolate the problem for a one-off solution. But dynamic, open environments make this impossible.

The Five Syndromes: (1) Lone Warrior, (2) Freeze the World, (3) Self-Made Box, (4) Rational High Ground, (5) Identity Around Established Practices — patterns preventing organizations from addressing complex problems.

Key Patterns

Conventional problem-solving fails not because practitioners are incompetent, but because problems have fundamentally changed. The five syndromes are deeply human patterns that become traps in complex, networked environments.

Practical Applications

Check for the five syndromes in your organization: Lone Warrior? Freeze the World? Self-Made Box? Rational High Ground? Identity around practices? Recognizing these patterns is the first step.

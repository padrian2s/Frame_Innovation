Chapter 3: Lessons from Design — Four Questions

Commentary

Summary

Four questions about design: How is it different from conventional problem-solving? What reasoning do designers use? How do they approach paradoxes? What can others learn? The chapter explores design as a distinct form of intelligence.

Key Patterns

Design reasoning is abductive and generative. The five practices (coevolution, problem situations, frames, themes, discourse) work as an integrated system. Expertise means comfort with complexity and ability to hold multiple frames.

Practical Applications

Practice abductive reasoning: work backward from desired outcomes. Instead of 'what can we do with what we have?' ask 'what would it look like if this were truly solved?' This reversal opens new possibilities.

Real-World Example

A tech startup with declining engagement: an analyst optimizes the funnel (working within the product frame). A designer questions whether the product frame itself is right — maybe users' needs evolved and the value proposition needs reimagining.

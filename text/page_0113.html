Running Effective Workshops

Commentary

Summary

Facilitation skills: maintaining openness, managing group dynamics, preventing premature convergence, recognizing promising themes and frames. Resist the group's natural rush to solutions.

Key Patterns

The nine-step model is both systematic and creative. Early steps build understanding; middle steps generate novelty; later steps ensure practicality. The process is iterative, not linear.

Practical Applications

Apply the nine steps: (1) Investigate history. (2) Identify tension. (3) Understand context. (4) Map stakeholders. (5) Find themes. (6) Propose frames. (7) Imagine consequences. (8) Test feasibility. (9) Plan integration.

Frames in Design Literature

Commentary

Summary

Schön's 'reflective conversation with the situation': designers propose frames, see what the situation 'talks back,' and adjust. Framing is iterative dialogue between proposals and responses, not a one-time act.

Reflective Practice: Schön's concept — a 'reflective conversation with the situation' where the designer proposes frames, the situation 'talks back,' and understanding adjusts iteratively.

Key Patterns

Design reasoning is abductive and generative. The five practices (coevolution, problem situations, frames, themes, discourse) work as an integrated system. Expertise means comfort with complexity and ability to hold multiple frames.

Practical Applications

Practice abductive reasoning: work backward from desired outcomes. Instead of 'what can we do with what we have?' ask 'what would it look like if this were truly solved?' This reversal opens new possibilities.

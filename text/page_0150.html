Chapter 7: The Three Challenges of Frame Innovation

Commentary

Summary

Three simultaneous shifts: seeing (perception), thinking (rationality), doing (practice). Each has obstacles. The world has set ways of perceiving, thinking, and acting that frame creation must challenge.

The Three Challenges: Seeing differently (shifting perception), Thinking differently (expanding rationality), Doing differently (changing practices).

Key Patterns

Seeing, thinking, and doing differently are interrelated. All three must be addressed. The challenges reflect deep assumptions about rationality, identity, and practice.

Practical Applications

Address three challenges simultaneously: create opportunities for seeing through others' eyes, introduce broader notions of valid reasoning, and protect experimental spaces for new approaches.

The Nine Steps of Frame Creation

Commentary

Summary

Nine steps: (1) Archaeology, (2) Paradox, (3) Context, (4) Field, (5) Themes, (6) Frames, (7) Futures, (8) Transformation, (9) Integration. Each builds on previous steps.

The Nine Steps: (1) Archaeology, (2) Paradox, (3) Context, (4) Field, (5) Themes, (6) Frames, (7) Futures, (8) Transformation, (9) Integration.

Key Patterns

The nine-step model is both systematic and creative. Early steps build understanding; middle steps generate novelty; later steps ensure practicality. The process is iterative, not linear.

Practical Applications

Apply the nine steps: (1) Investigate history. (2) Identify tension. (3) Understand context. (4) Map stakeholders. (5) Find themes. (6) Propose frames. (7) Imagine consequences. (8) Test feasibility. (9) Plan integration.

Real-World Example

Apply to university mental health: (1) How addressed before? (2) Students need support but stigma prevents seeking it. (3) Academic pressure, social media, financial stress. (4) Students, faculty, counselors, families. (5) Belonging, agency, purpose. (6) Campus as wellness ecosystem rather than 'mental health services.'

The Challenges Defined

Commentary

Summary

Dorst defines key terms. 'Open': unclear system boundaries, can't safely exclude factors. 'Complex': many elements with interdependent connections where small decisions create chain effects. These problems resist decomposition — severing relations creates new problems.

Open Problem: A problem with unclear or permeable system boundaries — you cannot safely exclude factors or stakeholders.
Complex Problem: Many elements with interdependent connections where small decisions create chain effects across seemingly unrelated areas.

Key Patterns

Conventional problem-solving fails not because practitioners are incompetent, but because problems have fundamentally changed. The five syndromes are deeply human patterns that become traps in complex, networked environments.

Practical Applications

Check for the five syndromes in your organization: Lone Warrior? Freeze the World? Self-Made Box? Rational High Ground? Identity around practices? Recognizing these patterns is the first step.

Real-World Example

Climate change: open (which emissions? which countries?), complex (energy, agriculture, transport, behavior, geopolitics), dynamic (technology, politics), networked (policy in China affects markets in Europe). No conventional approach can handle this.

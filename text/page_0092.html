Step 1: Archaeology

Commentary

Summary

Step 1: Archaeology — investigate the problem and all earlier attempts deeply. Uncover what happened AND what could have happened. Without examining alternative paths, your perceptions risk the same trap as the initial definition.

Archaeology (Step 1): Investigating the problem and all earlier attempts — including paths not taken — to understand history and avoid inherited assumptions.

Key Patterns

The nine-step model is both systematic and creative. Early steps build understanding; middle steps generate novelty; later steps ensure practicality. The process is iterative, not linear.

Practical Applications

Apply the nine steps: (1) Investigate history. (2) Identify tension. (3) Understand context. (4) Map stakeholders. (5) Find themes. (6) Propose frames. (7) Imagine consequences. (8) Test feasibility. (9) Plan integration.
